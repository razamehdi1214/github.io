About

I'm a fifth-year Ph.D. candidate in the Computational Cardiovascular Bioengineering Laboratory at Texas A&M University, advised by Dr. Reza Avazmohammadi. My work sits at the intersection of deep learning, medical imaging, and computational biomechanics.

My goal is to turn complex cardiac imaging data into non-invasive diagnostics — predicting where heart tissue is damaged, how stiff it has become, and where disease is heading, all without a catheter or biopsy. I do this by fusing physics-based simulation with modern AI, from UNets and transformers to physics-informed neural networks.

I'm actively seeking AI Research Scientist roles in healthcare, medical AI, and computational biology — where rigorous science and real patient impact go hand in hand.

Research Highlights
01
Multi-Fidelity Deep Learning for Myocardial Infarction Diagnosis

Built a UNet-style segmentation model that combines large-scale simulated rodent heart data with scarce human MRI labels to predict infarct location directly from left-ventricular strain maps — achieving accurate scar detection on small human MRI cohorts without LGE imaging.

Published · Advanced Science 2025 PyTorch · UNet · Transfer Learning
02
Physics-Informed Neural Networks for In Vivo Cardiac Mechanics

Designed a PINN embedding hyperelastic constitutive laws to predict myocardial stress-strain behavior and infer tissue material parameters from limited cardiac data. Combined PINN-derived tissue features with hemodynamics in a random-forest classifier — achieving perfect three-way separation of healthy, mild PH, and severe pulmonary hypertension.

Submitted · Under Review PINNs · Inverse Problems · FEM
03
Non-Invasive Pulmonary Artery Pressure Estimation

Developing a deep learning pipeline to automatically segment pulmonary vessels from high-resolution CT and extract morphological features for hemodynamic modeling. Training a physics-informed model on CT-derived features combined with FSI-generated pressures to predict distal pulmonary artery pressures without running full simulations.

In Preparation CT Segmentation · FSI · Transformer
04
LLMSurv: Explainable Deep Survival Modeling for Lung Transplant Outcomes

Developed an LLM-based explainable survival model for post-lung-transplant outcome prediction using the UNOS dataset, combining deep survival analysis with SHAP-based model explanations to surface clinically interpretable risk factors for transplant physicians.

Submitted · Under Review LLMs · Survival Analysis · SHAP
